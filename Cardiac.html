Congestive Heart Failure and
Cardiac Rehabilitation Study

This Retrospective Pilot Study assessed Cardiac Heart Failure (CHF) patients before and after completion of a Phase II 12-week Cardiac Rehabilitation program (Cardiac Rehab).

Text boxes in blue will clarifying and summarize the information detailed below. Look out for the lightbulb icon for plain language interpretation of the statistical data and terminologies.

Background & Demographics

Research Question:

Will there be a difference in CHF patient's knowledge, behavior (dietary and medication adherance), and outcomes (depression, metabolics, functional status, quality of life, CHF-related emergency visits, and CHF-related hospital readmissions etc.) after completing a 12-week Cardiac Rehab program?

Null Hypothesis (H0)

  • CHF patients who complete the Phase-II 12-week Cardiac Rehab program will demonstrate no differences in knowledge, behavior, and patient outcomes.

Alternate Hypothesis (HA)

  • CHF patients who complete the Phase-II 12-week Cardiac Rehab program will demonstrate differences in knowledge, behavior, and patient outcomes.

Study Subject Demographics (n = 21)

  • 57% (12) subjects were AHA Heart Failure classified III and IV.
  • Two-thirds (67%) of study subjects self-identified racially as white; and most study subjects (81%) self-identified their ethnicity as not Hispanic, Latino, or Spanish.
  • The study sample was predominately male (95%) with only 1 female.
  • 51% of subjects had Medicare or Medicaid as their primary insurance provider while 38% of subjects had Managed Care.

Study Sample

This sample achieved a calculated statistical power of 90% which exceeds the 80% statistical power threshold for sample sizes.

Statistical sampling power indicates how closely the sample aligns with the population of interest. Samples with > 80% statistical power demonstrates that the sample closely reflects the population; therefore, any analysis and conclusions based on the sample would reflect that of the population.

Twenty-one CHF patients met study elegibility criteria (Total = 55).

Eligibility Criteria

  • ≥18 years old
  • History of CHF diagnosis
  • Class II-IV Heart Failure (as per the American Heart Association (AHA))
  • Systolic dysfunction (not exclusive of combined dysfunction) + ejection fraction ≤ 35%
  • no planned/scheduled major medical interventions
  • optimized 6-week medical therapy
  • completed the Phase II 12-week Cardiac Rehab program

Comparison of Scores: Pre and Post Completion of the Phase II 12-Week Cardiac Rehabilitation Program

Matched-Pairs t-Tests (two-tailed) were conducted to compare the difference in the average scores of the paired study endpoints collected from CHF patients before and after completion of the Cardiac Rehab program.

This analysis measured the difference (positive or negative) in patient outcomes before and after completing Cardiac Rehab.

Patient Outcomes of Interest for this study measured before/after Cardiac Rehab.

  • Knowledge & Behavior via Atlanta Heart Failure Knowledge Test V2 Questionnaire
  • Dietary & Medication Adherance via Rate My Plate
  • Depression via Patient Health Questionnaire-9 Depression Scale
  • Metabolics via Metabolic Equivalent of Task scores
  • Functional Status via Dartmouth COOP Functional Health Assessment Charts/WONCA
  • Quality of Life via Minnesota Living with Heart Failure Questionnaire
  • CHF-Related ETC
  • CHF-related Hospital Readmissions 30-, 60-, and 180-days before/after Cardiac Rehab

Note: Statistical significance is impacted by the size of the sample. A larger sample size for comparison could provide a more precise analysis and thereby impact the statistical significance.

Statistical sigificance means that the results found are real and not just a fluke or due to chance. It gives us confidence that the differences observed in data are meaningful and not random.

P-values indicate to what % of confidence the results are real. A p-value of < 0.05 translates to 95% or more confidence that results are real.

Note: Statistical significance is important, but real world significance is important too. Clinical significance demonstrates that the results showed clinical relevance and meaningful impact for the patients, even if the statistical significance was not demonstrated.

Atlanta Heart Failure Knowledge Test V2 (AHFKT) Questionnaire

CHF patients demonstrated increased knowledge of disease management & improved disease management behavior after completing Cardiac Rehab.

Study participants demonstrated increased knowledge and behavior related to disease management after completing the Cardiac Rehab Program. The difference in the average AHFKT scores (3.39) was clinically and statistically significant (p-value 0.0027).

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Higher scores indicate improved patient outcomes

The 30-item AHFKT measures knowledge and behavior related to heart failure disease management. Each item is scored as correct or incorrect and the total number of correct answers are calculated and presented as a total sum (30 points) or total percentage (100%) (Butts, Higgins, Dunbar, & Reilly, 2018).

Dartmouth COOP Functional Health Assessment Charts/WONCA (DCFHA/WONCA)

CHF patients demonstrated increased functional health status after completing Cardiac Rehab. Functional status is defined as physical and emotional function, daily activities, social activities, social support, change in health, overall health, pain, quality of life, etc. - all of which contribute to understanding one's quality of life.

Patients who completed Cardiac Rehab program had lower DCFHA/WONCA scores which demonstrates their improved functional health status. The difference in the average DCFHA/WONCA scores (-3.9) was both clinically significant and statistically significant (p-value 0.0014).

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Lower scores indicate improved patient outcomes

The DCFHA/WONCA measures the functional health status providing information on somatic, psychological and social functional capacity (Bentsen, Natvig, Winnem, 1999). Each of the nine charts have a 5-point scale.

Rate My Plate (RMP)

CHF patients demonstrated improvement their eating habits specifically frequency of meals and adherance to dietary constraints after completing Cardiac Rehab.

At completion of the Cardiac Rehab program, the increase in RMP scores demonstrated patients' improved eating habits related to food frequency and dietary adherance. While clinically significant, the difference in average RMP scores (1.24) was not statistically significant (p-value >0.05)

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Higher scores indicate improved patient outcomes

The 27-item RMP assesses food-frequency and dietary adherence. Each item scores on a 3-point scale. Scores between 27-45 indicate that there are many ways to improve eating habits, scores 46-63 indicate some ways to improve eating habits, and scores 64-81 indicate many healthy choices are made.

Minnesota Living with Heart Failure Questionnaire (MLHFQ)

CHF patients demonstrated improved emotional and physical quality of life after completing Cardiac Rehab.

Patients who completed Cardiac Rehab scored lower average MLHFQ scores within the good quality of life - the highest possible level for this tool. The difference in average MLHFQ scores (-8.09) was clinically and statistically significant (p-value 0.04524).

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Lower scores indicate improved patient outcomes

The 21-item MLHFQ measures physical and emotional dimensions of health-related quality of life for patients with heart failure. Each item scores on a 6-point Likert Scale (0-5). Scores less than 24 indicate good quality of life, scores between 24-45 indicate moderate quality of life, and scores greater than 45 indicate poor quality of life (Behlouli et al., 2009)

Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) Depression Scale

CHF patients demonstrated lower depression severity scores after completing Cardiac Rehab.

At completion of the Cardiac Rehab program, the average PHQ-9 score of 3.29 was lower compared to the average PHQ-9 score of 4.62 measured before completing the Cardiac Rehab program. While clinically significant, the difference in average PHQ-9 scores (-1.33) was not statistically significant (p-value >0.05).

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Lower scores indicate reduced depression severity.

The self-administered PHQ-9 measures the severity of depression present in the respondent. This 9-item questionnaire assigns a value of 0 to 3 points per item. Depression severity is indicated as minimal when scores are between 0-4, mild when scored between 5-9, moderate when scored 10-14, moderately severe when scored 15-19, and severe when scored 20-27 (Kroenke, Spitzer, & Williams, 2001).

Metabolic Equivalent of Task (MET) Scores

CHF patients demonstrated increased metabolic rates - indicating greater levels of activity/exercise within the moderate activity range - after completing Cardiac Rehab.

At completion of the Cardiac Rehab program, the average MET score of 4.14 was higher compared to the average MET score of 3.08 measured before completing the Cardiac Rehab program. The difference in average MET scores (1.06) was both clinically and statistically significant (p-value 0.0002).

Reject the null hypothesis (SAS 9.4)

Higher scores indicate improved patient outcomes

MET is a ratio of working metabolic rate relative or compared to resting metabolic rates. Metabolic rates indicate the rate of expended energy over time and indicates the intensity of an activity or exercise. Scores of 1 MET indicate the energy used when resting or sitting while an activity of 4 MET is equivalent to briskly walking at 3-4 miles per hour. Light intensity activities are indicated by less than 3 MET; moderate intensity activities are 3-6 MET, and vigorous intensity activities are greater than or equal to 6 MET (Haskell et al, 2007).

Patient Outcomes: CHF-Related Readmissions and Emergency Visits

Descriptive analysis and comparative summaries were used to describe the Emergency & Trauma Center (ETC) visits and 30-, 60-, and 180-day hospital readmission rates before and after completion of the cardiac rehab program.

  • Of the 21 subjects enrolled in the study, 13 subjects (17 encounters) experienced a CHF-related ETC visit and/or hospital readmission before or after completion of the cardiac rehab program.
  • Study findings indicate that CHF-related 30-, 60-, and 180-day hospital readmissions decreased among subjects who completed the Phase II 12-week Cardiac Rehabilitation. Reject the null hypothesis.

CHF-related ETC Visits

Two CHF patients went to the Emergency & Trauma Center (ETC) for CHF-related diagnosis after completing Cardiac Rehab compared to the one CHF patient who went to the ETC before the program.

Why? Statistically speaking, this can be due to the small sample size causing random fluctuations (a larger sample size would "smooth out" the impact of fluctiations in the data). Furthermore, there is no indicated relationship between Cardiac Rehab and the patients' ETC visits.

Note that this study did not assess patients' non-cardiac characteristics/comorbidities that may have impacted the patients' overall health status and contributed to the ETC visit.

In conclusion, this study could not directly link Cardiac Rehab to CHF-related ETC visits. Further study and a larger sample size would be needed to further explore this variable.

Study findings indicate that CHF-related ETC visits increased 50% among subjects who completed the Phase II 12-week Cardiac Rehabilitation program (CR).

Evidence Based Practice Project
Project Approval Process

This process improvement project utilized Lean Six Sigma (LSS) to streamline the approval process for evidence-based practice (EBP) projects for clinical nurses in a healthcare institution.

Text boxes in blue will clarifying and summarize the information detailed below. Look out for the lightbulb icon for plain language interpretation of the analytical data and terminologies.

Background

Anecdotal evidence indicated that the pre-implementation process for EBP project development and subsquent approval was long and arduous. Most frequently, time was a pain point in the process followed second by confusion regarding the EBP project approval criteria at the [Organization].

Establishing an efficient approval process for Evidence-Based Practice (EBP) Projects is crucial for ensuring that these projects are properly vetted and implemented in a timely manner. Given the need for clarity in the EBP approval process and the challenges of meeting time-sensitive deadlines, addressing the low approval rate and lengthly timelines for project completion was essential.

Setting: The setting for this project was within a university medical center identified as the [Organization].

The [Organization] proved an ideal setting due to accessibility to populations of interest and opportunities for direct application of evidence-based knowledge into clinical settings. Note that all research and EBP were conducted within the scope and oversight of governing regulatory bodies for evidence-based care, transformational research, and human subject protection to name a few.

Consequently, all EBP projects were required complete the EBP pre-implementation process as outlined below:

EBP Pre-Implementation Process

  1. EBP Preliminary - request support from [Institute]
  2. EBP Project Development - summarize the problem, the intervention, and supporting evidence
  3. Action plan development - outline the project proposal including resources, logistics & leadership support
  4. [Review Committee] Submission - submit the EBP Project evidence and plan for review
  5. [Review Committee] Recommendations - if not approved, feedback and recommendations are provided
  6. Implementation Recommendation - if approved, the EBP project lead proceeds to implementation

During pre-implementation, EBP projects are developed in collabroation with the [Institute]. Nurses conceptualize their EBP project ideas with support from the [Institute] Nurse Scientists to develop an implementation plan and summarize supporting evidence.

EBP project development entails summarizing the problem, describing proposed intervention, presenting evidence from literary and/or regulatory sources, and presenting an action plan for implementation of the EBP project on a population of interest.

Project Summary - Process Improvement

Lean Six Sigma (LSS) methodology utilizing the DMAIC Model was conducted to assess current state in order to refine and streamline the EBP approval process.

The DMAIC model identifies the problem, measures current state, analyzes root cause of the problems, implements solutions, and verifies whether results align with intended target.

Once proven effective in addressing the issue, the model pivots towards sustaining and improving the new process.

DMAIC Model

  • Define the problem
  • Measure - quantify the problem
  • Analyze - identify the cause of the problem
  • Improve - implement and verify the proposed solution
  • Control - maintain the solution

Lean Six Sigma (LSS) - DMAIC Model

Define

Step 1: Define

Define the problem and focus on clearly identifying the problem while clarifying the project scope by utilizing of a project charter.

Project Focus
  • Refine the approval process for EBP projects.
Problem
  • Opportunity for improvement in the EBP project approval process

The project received sponsorship from the Administrative Director for the [Institute]. A steering committee for the project was created and members comprised of of two nurse scientists, one research coordinator, and the Administrative Direct for the [Institute].

The scope of the project is limited only to EBP projects submitted for review to the [Institute] and [Review Committee] and excludes any projects not submitted to the [Institute] and [Review Committee].

Primary Measures

  • Quality - Number of EBP projects meeting critera and receive approval at initial submission for review.
  • Time - Number of EBP projects having timely progression to approval and implementation

Secondary Measures

  • Speed of project completion
  • Consistency in application of new/standardized process
  • Time for correcting errors

Goal

  • Increase annual number of completed EBP projects from 1 per year to 1 per month
  • Increase percentage of approved projects from 20% to 50%
  • Increase number of projects receiving approval the first time they're presented for approval

Opportunities

Improve efficiency of EBP approval process, reduce wasted time, and increase timely progression of EBP projects through the approval process

Problem: low number of approved EBP projects (20% approved/year)

Measure

Step 2: Measure

Assess the targeted problem by thoroughly analyzing the process outcomes to identify areas of waste, bottlenecks, and improvement oppportunities. Data on key process metrics are collected, including wasted time, cycle time, and reasons for delay.

Process maps were created to visualize the current workflow and identify areas of inefficiency.

The five EBP projects that had completed the pre-implementation process in 2018 were assessed; specifically through measurement of time to completion/approval.

  • Touch time (TT) indicated the amount of time the EBP project lead and/or [Institute] team member actively worked on the project.
  • Wait Time (WT) demonstrates the duration of time the project waited without progressing to the next phase.
  • Right the First Time (RFT) outlines the percentage of time the project was approved upon initial submission.

Wait-time was identified as substantial reason for delays in the EBP project process

Measure Summary - Reasons for Process Delays

  1. EBP Project development and refinement phases (1-4) take too long
  2. EBP Project revision wait time is too long
  3. Incomplete submissions and projects increase cycle time

Analysis

Step 3: Analysis

The root causes identfied are now analyzed to identify barriers, inefficiencies or areas of opportunities. Fishbone diagrams and Root Cause analysis were utilized to identify areas for improvement and key process failures.

The steering committee conducted three brainstorming sessions to isolate the key reasons for delays in the EBP pre-implementation process. They were subsequently sorted into 6 key areas:

  1. People - Knowledge/Skills
  2. Materials - Information/Supplies
  3. Measurement - Progress/Timeliness
  4. Environment - Clinical/Organizational Environment
  5. Method - Process & Systems
  6. Tools - Documents and Structure

Six Key Areas of Improvement Analyzed

Two Targeted Areas for Improvement

Although there were six key areas for improvement identified by the steering committee, only two could be influenced or changed within the scope of control afforded by the scope of this process improvement project.

Improve

Step 4: Improve

During this phase, solutions were developed and implemented to address the identified root causes. Subsequently, timely communication was prioritized, exisiting structures were refined, and targeted education was provided to [Institute] team members.

Recommendations & Process Interventions
The finalized improvement strategies included the following three categories:

  1. Automatic Reminders
  2. Refine Process & Utilize Checklists
  3. Ongoing Knowledge & Skill Development
Automatic Reminders - Leveraging Existing Technology
  • Workfront - Automatic reminders to check in with EBP project leader and prompt for next steps and incomplete components
  • Outlook - Send immediate meeting summaries to EBP project leader, schedule next meeting in current meetings, and
Accountability & Tracking -
  • Outline the EBP project pre-implementation process step by step
  • Specify tasks for EBP project leader and [Institute] team member
  • Ensure all components and attachments are received
  • Verify content for completion and accuracy
  • Develop EBP project with minimal content errors and increased time efficiency
  • Decreased number of EBP project requiring major revisions by the [Review Committee]
Ongoing Knowledge & Skill Development - Education & Refreshers
  • Ongoing education on the EBP project development and refinement process
  • Attend educational sessions and utilize departmental sessions to review EBP pre-implementation process
  • Consistent application of knowledge and expertise

Project Summary

The project resulted in several key outcomes. First, the refined process demonstrated less wasted time in the process and this is further quantified in dollars by assessing the cost of reduced wasted time.

Second, clarifying the EBP approval criteria increased the likelihood that EBP projects would receive approval after completing only one review.

Goal

  • Increase number of EBP projects completed to 1 project per month
  • 50% of projects receiving approval the first time it is presented
Results/Outcome
Only one EBP project met criteria within the duration of the project; nevertheless, the outcomes were excellent.
  • 80% of projects received approval the first time it was presented for approval
  • Cycle time reduced from 197 days to 44 days
  • Touch Time reduced from 23 days to 2.5 days

Average Touch Time Savings (#) = 20.5 days * 8hrs = 164 work hours per project
Average Touch Time Cost Savings ($) = 164 work hours * $65 per hour (average rate) = $10,660 per project
